About me

I was born in Brazil in 1968 and moved to the US in 2022, where I am now pursuing a career in web development using my current and prior experiences in Design and Education fields. Both professional and academic experience helped me shape the way I design things.
I am currently an Adjunct Instructor at University of Cincinnati. I am a Game Design, Web & Graphic Design and Concept Art Associate Professor at the Institute of Arts and Design of the Federal University of Juiz de Fora, a public university in Brazil, currently on unpaid leave. My 12 years teaching experience came from 31 years of experience in the Brazilian gaming industry as web & graphic designer, concept artist, art director and game designer for narrative games, especially tRPGs.
I was a Design Consultant at the UNESCO Chair in Reading at PUC-Rio and worked for 19 years as a Graphic Design and Illustration professional. My education includes a Graduate degree in Art Theory at the Rio de Janeiro State University (1998), a Master's (2002) and a PhD (2008) in Design at the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro.
At the moment (2023), I am pursuing a certificate as Web Developer at Kable Academy and earned a CIW Site Development Associate certificate.

What I do

HOW

I DESIGN IMAGES and MEDIA using LudoPoetics, a method I developed based on Roland Barthes Semiotics to instigate critical thinking. My tools are Adobe Creative Suite, Procreate, HTML/ CSS/ Bootstrap, JavaScript, Google Apps and Minecraft. My GitHub: https://github.com/elianebettocchi

WHAT FOR

I TEACH using a didatic approach inspired by bell hooks: rescuing the traditional knowledge of the original peoples as a means to contribute to the continuity of society, transgressing and then improving it. My tools are Google Apps, Canvas, Wordpress and Microsoft Office.

WHY

I RESEARCH the potential of narrative games as poetic expression and means of changing the world by addressing diversity and representation inspired by Lindsay Grace. My tools are Decolonial Studies, Semiotics, participant observation and discourse analysis.
