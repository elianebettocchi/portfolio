About Me

About me

I was born in Brazil in 1968 and moved to the US in 2022, where I am now an Adjunct Instructor at University of Cincinnati. I am a Game Design, Graphic Design and Concept Art Associate Professor at the Institute of Arts and Design of the Federal University of Juiz de Fora, a public university in Brazil, currently on unpaid leave. My 12 years teaching experience came from 31 years of experience in the Brazilian gaming industry as graphic designer, concept artist, art director and game designer for narrative games, especially tRPGs. I was a Design Consultant at the UNESCO Chair in Reading at PUC-Rio and worked for 19 years as a Graphic Design and Illustration professional. I have a Graduate degree in Art Theory at the Rio de Janeiro State University (1998), a Master's (2002) and a PhD (2008) in Design at the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro. Both professional and academic experience helped me shape the way I design things.

What I do

What I do

HOW

I DESIGN IMAGES and MEDIA using LudoPoetics, a method I developed based on Roland Barthes Semiotics to instigate (Design as Advertisement) critical thinking (Design as Denouncement).

WHAT FOR

I TEACH using a didatic approach inspired by bell hooks: rescuing the traditional knowledge of the original peoples as a means to contribute to the continuity of society, transgressing and then improving it.

WHY

I RESEARCH the potential of narrative games as poetic expression and means of changing the world by addressing diversity and representation inspired by Lindsay Grace.
